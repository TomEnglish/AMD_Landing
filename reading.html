Key Researchers

Dr. Ray Peat

Physiologist & Researcher

Pioneering work on thyroid, metabolism, and the dangers of polyunsaturated fats. His framework connecting cellular respiration to chronic disease underlies much of the metabolic approach.

Dr. Nicolas Bazan

Neuroscience Center, LSU

Discovered neuroprotectin D1 (NPD1), the protective molecule synthesized from DHA that helps protect retinal cells from oxidative damage and inflammation.

Prof. Michael Crawford

Imperial College London

Decades of research on DHA and brain evolution. His work connecting human cognitive development to shoreline habitation explains the retina's dependence on marine nutrition.

Dr. Michael Hamblin

Harvard Medical School

Leading researcher in photobiomodulation. His work on red and near-infrared light therapy provides the mechanistic foundation for light-based AMD interventions.

Dr. Chris Knobbe

Ophthalmologist & Researcher

Author of "Ancestral Dietary Strategy to Prevent and Treat Macular Degeneration." Directly addresses the role of seed oils in AMD development.

Dr. Yandell Henderson

Yale Physiologist (1873-1944)

Pioneer in understanding carbon dioxide's role in oxygen delivery. His work on the Bohr effect and tissue oxygenation remains relevant to understanding retinal blood flow.
