A Different Perspective

Drusen—the yellow deposits that accumulate beneath the retina in age-related macular degeneration—share a remarkable molecular fingerprint with arterial plaques. Both contain the same lipids, cholesterol, and inflammatory proteins. Both form in tissues experiencing chronic stress and compromised blood flow.

Yet mainstream treatment focuses almost exclusively on the final stage: injecting drugs to stop abnormal blood vessel growth. The underlying metabolic dysfunction goes unaddressed.

This book presents a different framework—one grounded in peer-reviewed research but largely ignored by conventional ophthalmology: AMD is fundamentally a metabolic and vascular condition.
